-------- Book 1 --------

--- Chapter 1 ---

Building No-28 is an unregistered underground complex located in the [REDACTED] region.
Official records do not acknowledge its existence. Civilian archives list the location as restricted industrial ruins.
In reality, it is a high-command facility controlled primarily by upper government authorities and private financial backers
with influence powerful enough to erase anything they consider inconvenient.

While Starlight Facility was created to study the STARS phenomenon in hopes of understanding it,
preventing further disasters, and possibly preserving what remains of humanity,
Building No-28 was founded under a very different idea:
that STARS were not a warning,
not something sacred,
not something beyond human control-
but something that could be turned into profit, influence, leverage, and weaponry.

Inside No-28, officials did not treat STARS as cosmic anomalies.
They treated them as valuable resources.

Over time, No-28 became a silent marketplace of things that should never have been touched.
Captured fragments of STAR energy,
stolen research from other facilities,
classified organic materials taken from destroyed cities,
and even living subjects labeled “non-civilian biological entities”
were stored, experimented on, traded, and occasionally sold
to militaries, corporations, and unnamed private investors
whose identities are permanently erased from all databases.

In open meetings and public records,
the government spoke of “national stability,” “research progress,” and “public safety.”
Behind sealed steel doors and under false documentation,
Building No-28 authorized experiments,
containments,
and negotiations
that placed humanity at greater risk than any honest scientist ever would have allowed.

This is the fundamental reason Starlight Facility despises Building No-28.

Starlight scientists attempted to warn commanding officials that STARS were not a controllable discovery,
but an existential unknown capable of rewriting reality itself.
Officials inside No-28 laughed at the warnings.
To them,
STARS were simply a new frontier of wealth.
Where Starlight saw danger, destruction, and fragile stability,
No-28 saw opportunities, groundbreaking advantages,
and something humanity could “own” if they tried hard enough.

The conflict between the two entities never became a war,
but it was close.

Starlight scientists accused No-28 leadership of endangering the world for personal gain.
No-28 leaders accused Starlight of being “overdramatic” and “self-righteous”
and repeatedly attempted to access Starlight restricted knowledge,
claiming government authority required “shared responsibility of power.”
Starlight refused,
stating plainly in internal communication logs
that Building No-28 was not simply reckless,
but actively dangerous to the survival of humanity.

These tensions only deepened when Building No-28 began secretly detaining individuals:
failed experiment subjects,
civilians who had seen too much,
and people connected to STAR incidents that the government wanted erased.
Many of the detainees were never logged as missing.
Their families never received confirmation of death.
Their existence was rewritten to nothing.

Publicly,
authorities claimed that no human rights violations existed inside Building No-28.
Private internal records tell a very different story.

Lower levels of the facility contain isolated containment corridors,
medical rooms that do not resemble hospitals,
and reinforced cells designed not just to imprison,
but to break.

Not for research.
Not for justice.
For control.

And profit.

Funding for the facility comes from wealthy corporate figures,
military benefactors,
and high-ranked officials
who treat STARS as a new economy waiting to be opened,
rather than a warning humanity should listen to.
These backers never appear in civilian history.
They exist only in redacted documents,
coded financial reports,
and silent agreements.

Building No-28 is not recognized as a threat by the public,
because to the public
it doesn’t exist at all.

But Starlight Facility understands what it truly is:
not a laboratory,
not an ally,
but a selfish creation of humanity’s worst instincts,
motivated not by survival,
but by greed and arrogance.

One day,
when everything finally reaches its breaking point,
the truth of Building No-28 will matter.

Until then,
it continues to function,
continue to collect,
continue to harm,
and continue to wait.
